Referral System

A referral system is an essential part of maternal healthcare. It ensures that pregnant women who need specialized or emergency care are promptly transferred to higher-level health facilities.

Timely referral helps prevent complications, reduces maternal and newborn mortality, and ensures that both mother and baby receive appropriate care when needed.

What Is a Referral System?

The referral system connects primary healthcare facilities—such as Barangay Health Stations and Rural Health Units—to higher-level hospitals with advanced medical services.

  • Ensures continuity of care across different health facilities
  • Allows early management of high-risk pregnancies
  • Provides access to specialized doctors, equipment, and emergency care
  • Improves outcomes for both mother and baby

When Is Referral Needed?

A pregnant woman may be referred to a higher-level facility if certain risk factors or complications are identified during antenatal visits.

  • High blood pressure or signs of preeclampsia
  • Severe anemia or bleeding
  • Preterm labor or prolonged labor
  • Abnormal fetal heart rate or reduced fetal movements
  • Multiple pregnancy (twins or more)
  • Existing medical conditions such as diabetes or heart disease

Emergency Referrals

Emergency referrals are critical situations that require immediate transfer to a hospital with emergency obstetric and newborn care services.

  • Severe vaginal bleeding
  • Convulsions or loss of consciousness
  • Severe abdominal pain
  • Signs of infection such as high fever
  • Obstructed or prolonged labor

In emergencies, transportation should be arranged immediately, and the receiving facility should be informed in advance whenever possible.

Role of Healthcare Providers

Healthcare providers play a key role in ensuring a smooth referral process. They assess the condition, explain the reason for referral, and coordinate care between facilities.

  • Early identification of risks and complications
  • Clear communication with the mother and family
  • Proper documentation and referral notes
  • Follow-up after referral to ensure continuity of care

A well-functioning referral system saves lives. Pregnant women and their families should always follow referral advice and seek care at the recommended facility to ensure the safest possible outcome for both mother and baby.
