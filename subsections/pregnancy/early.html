Early Registration of Pregnancy

Early registration of pregnancy is an important first step in ensuring a healthy pregnancy for both the mother and the baby. Antenatal Care (ANC) should begin as soon as pregnancy is suspected.

Every woman of reproductive age is encouraged to visit a health provider if she believes she is pregnant. Ideally, the first antenatal visit should take place within the first 12 weeks of pregnancy.

Even if a woman registers later in pregnancy, she should still receive appropriate care based on her gestational age. The Department of Health (DOH) promotes early registration through Barangay Health Stations and Rural Health Units.

Timing of the First Visit

The first antenatal visit or pregnancy registration should occur as early as possible to allow timely assessment and planning.

  • Register as soon as pregnancy is suspected
  • Ideally within the first 12 weeks of pregnancy
  • Late registration should still receive full antenatal care
  • Care is adjusted based on the stage of pregnancy

Importance of Early Detection

Early detection of pregnancy allows healthcare providers to identify potential risks and provide proper guidance from the beginning.

  • Supports proper planning and continuous antenatal care
  • Allows early assessment of the mother’s health status
  • Helps record baseline data such as blood pressure, weight, and hemoglobin
  • Enables early identification and management of complications

Pregnancy Dating and Monitoring

During early registration, important pregnancy details are recorded to guide care throughout pregnancy.

  • Recording the Last Menstrual Period (LMP)
  • Calculating the Expected Date of Delivery (EDD)
  • Establishing baseline health measurements
  • Planning follow-up visits and tests

Building Trust and Support

Early registration helps build a strong relationship between the pregnant woman and her healthcare provider.

When care begins early, women are more likely to communicate their needs, concerns, and preferences, which helps in planning a safe delivery and positive birth experience.

Folic Acid Supplementation

All women of reproductive age are advised to take folic acid before and during early pregnancy to reduce the risk of birth defects.

  • Take folic acid 2–3 months before conception if possible
  • Continue folic acid during the first 12 weeks of pregnancy
  • Recommended daily dose: 400 μg taken orally
  • Significantly reduces neural tube defects in the fetus

Early registration of pregnancy ensures that mothers receive the right care at the right time. It lays the foundation for a healthy pregnancy, safe delivery, and positive outcomes for both mother and baby.
