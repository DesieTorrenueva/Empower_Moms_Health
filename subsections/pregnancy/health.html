Health Assessment & Monitoring

Health assessment and monitoring are essential parts of antenatal care. They help healthcare providers evaluate the mother’s overall health, monitor the baby’s development, and detect potential risks early in pregnancy.

Regular assessments allow timely interventions, proper management of conditions, and referrals when necessary to ensure a safe pregnancy and delivery.

Monitoring the Mother’s Health

During antenatal visits, healthcare providers routinely check the mother’s vital signs and physical condition to identify any health concerns.

  • Blood pressure monitoring to detect hypertension or pre-eclampsia
  • Weight measurement to track healthy weight gain
  • Assessment of anemia through hemoglobin testing
  • Monitoring blood sugar levels to screen for gestational diabetes
  • Checking for signs of infection or other medical conditions

Monitoring the Baby’s Well-Being

Health assessment also focuses on ensuring the baby is growing and developing normally inside the womb.

  • Measuring fundal height to assess fetal growth
  • Listening to the fetal heartbeat
  • Monitoring fetal movements as pregnancy progresses
  • Ultrasound examinations to check fetal development and position

Identifying Risks Early

Early detection of health risks allows healthcare providers to manage conditions before they become serious.

  • Identification of high-risk pregnancies
  • Early management of medical conditions such as hypertension or diabetes
  • Timely referral to higher-level facilities if complications arise
  • Planning for a safe delivery based on the mother’s health status

Consistent health assessment and monitoring help protect both mother and baby. Attending all scheduled antenatal visits and following medical advice are key steps toward a healthy pregnancy and safe childbirth.
