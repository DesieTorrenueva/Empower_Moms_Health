Healthy Tips in the Second Trimester (13–26 Weeks)

The second trimester is often called the most comfortable stage of pregnancy because energy levels improve and baby growth becomes more noticeable.

Nutrition and Weight Gain

  • Eat a balanced diet with slightly increased calories
  • Include calcium-rich foods for bone development
  • Eat iron-rich foods to help prevent anemia
  • Continue taking prenatal vitamins

Focus on:

  • Iron-rich foods
  • Calcium and vitamin D
  • Fiber for healthy digestion

Increase calories modestly — about 340 extra calories per day for most women.

Physical Activity

  • Engage in moderate exercise such as walking, swimming, or prenatal yoga with doctor’s approval
  • Practice pelvic floor (Kegel) exercises
  • Maintain good posture; supportive shoes help reduce back and joint discomfort

Activity safety: Avoid activities with a risk of falling or abdominal injury. Consult your healthcare provider if you have complications.

Body Care and Comfort

  • Wear comfortable clothing and supportive shoes
  • Sleep on your side for better blood circulation
  • Stretch regularly to ease muscle tension

Health Monitoring

  • Attend regular prenatal check-ups
  • Monitor weight and blood pressure
  • Follow screening tests as advised by your healthcare provider
  • Discuss glucose screening for gestational diabetes if recommended
  • Ask about appropriate weight gain for your health
