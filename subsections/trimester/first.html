Healthy Tips in the First Trimester (0–12 Weeks)

The first trimester is a critical stage of pregnancy because your baby’s organs are forming. Good nutrition, adequate rest, and early prenatal care are very important to support healthy development.

Nutrition and Vitamins

  • Take prenatal vitamins daily, especially folic acid, to prevent birth defects
  • Eat small, frequent meals to help reduce nausea
  • Include fruits, vegetables, whole grains, eggs, lean meat, and dairy products
  • Drink plenty of water to prevent dehydration

Calorie note: Most women do not need extra calories yet in this stage, but your body is already working hard to support your baby.

Avoid Harmful Substances

  • Avoid alcohol, smoking, and vaping — no amount is considered safe
  • Limit caffeine intake
  • Avoid raw or undercooked foods and unpasteurized dairy products

Activity and Rest

  • Get enough rest and quality sleep
  • Do light physical activities such as walking or gentle stretching
  • Avoid heavy lifting and strenuous exercise
  • Aim for at least 150 minutes per week of moderate exercise with doctor approval
  • Light stretching and pelvic floor exercises (Kegels) can improve comfort and preparation

Prenatal Care

  • Schedule your first prenatal check-up as early as possible
  • Follow medical advice regarding supplements and tests
  • Report severe vomiting, bleeding, or pain immediately
