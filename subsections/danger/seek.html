Seek Immediate Medical Help

During pregnancy, some symptoms require urgent medical attention. These warning signs may indicate serious complications that can affect both the mother and the baby. Acting quickly can prevent life-threatening outcomes.

Contact your midwife or obstetrician immediately, or go to the nearest hospital or emergency facility if you experience any of the following symptoms.

Emergency Warning Signs

  • Vaginal bleeding or severe, persistent stomach pain
  • Sudden change or decrease in your baby’s movements
  • Severe headache accompanied by blurred vision, nausea, vomiting, or swelling of the face and hands
  • Extreme emotional distress, panic attacks, or thoughts of harming yourself or your baby
  • Red, swollen, hot, or painful calf, which may indicate a blood clot
  • Pain or burning when urinating, fever, chills, or unusually frequent urination
  • Stomach tightening that feels regular or painful, which may be signs of preterm labor

Why Immediate Action Matters

Delaying medical care can increase the risk of complications such as infection, preterm birth, severe bleeding, or pregnancy loss. Many serious conditions can be treated effectively when identified early.

Never worry about “overreacting.” Healthcare providers would rather assess a symptom early than treat a serious emergency later.

Always trust your instincts. If something feels unusual, painful, or worrying, seek medical help immediately. Your health and your baby’s safety are always the priority.
