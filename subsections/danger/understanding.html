Understanding Serious Symptoms

Understanding specific danger signs helps you recognize when symptoms may indicate a serious complication. Early recognition allows faster treatment and better outcomes for both mother and baby.

Bleeding from the Vagina

Vaginal bleeding during pregnancy can range from light spotting to heavy bleeding. While some causes are harmless, others require urgent medical care.

First Trimester (0–12 Weeks)

  • Implantation bleeding (light spotting)
  • Hormonal changes affecting the cervix
  • Threatened miscarriage
  • Miscarriage
  • Ectopic pregnancy
  • Molar pregnancy

Second & Third Trimester (13–40 Weeks)

  • Late miscarriage (12–23 weeks)
  • Cervical infection or inflammation
  • Placenta praevia
  • Placental abruption
  • Signs of labor or premature labor (before 37 weeks)

Any bleeding should be reported to your healthcare provider immediately.

Stomach Pain

Mild cramps can be normal as the uterus grows. However, severe, sudden, or persistent pain may signal a serious problem.

  • Urinary tract infection
  • Constipation or trapped gas
  • Preterm or premature labor
  • Placental complications

Seek medical care if the pain is intense, worsening, or accompanied by bleeding or fever.

Change in Baby’s Movements

Babies develop a regular movement pattern. Any noticeable decrease, increase, or change may indicate distress.

  • Low amniotic fluid
  • Placental problems
  • Restricted baby growth
  • Risk of stillbirth

Contact your healthcare provider immediately if you notice reduced movements.

Shortness of Breath or Chest Pain

Mild breathlessness is common in later pregnancy due to pressure on the lungs. However, sudden or severe breathing difficulty is an emergency.

Chest pain, rapid breathing, or sharp pain when inhaling may indicate a blood clot or heart problem and requires immediate medical attention.
