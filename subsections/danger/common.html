Common Warning Signs

During pregnancy, your body goes through many changes. While some discomforts are normal, certain symptoms may signal a developing complication. Recognizing these common warning signs allows you to seek care before serious problems occur.

It is not always easy to tell the difference between normal pregnancy changes and warning signs. If you feel unsure or worried, always consult your midwife or obstetrician.

Common Warning Signs to Watch For

  • Vaginal bleeding: May range from light spotting to heavy bleeding and can occur at any stage of pregnancy.
  • Persistent or severe stomach pain: Pain that does not improve or worsens may indicate infection, preterm labor, or placental problems.
  • Shortness of breath or chest pain: Sudden or severe breathing difficulty should never be ignored.
  • Swelling of the face, hands, feet, or ankles: Sudden or severe swelling may be linked to high blood pressure or preeclampsia.
  • Emotional distress or mental health changes: Persistent sadness, anxiety, panic, or feelings of hopelessness may require professional support.
  • Changes in baby’s movements: Reduced, absent, or sudden changes in movement patterns should be checked immediately.

Some warning signs may appear gradually, while others happen suddenly. Paying attention to your body and your baby’s movements is essential throughout pregnancy.

Never delay seeking help. Early medical attention can prevent complications and protect both mother and baby.
