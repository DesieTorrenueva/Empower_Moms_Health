Healthy Tips by Trimester

Pregnancy is a time of major physical, emotional, and lifestyle changes. While many changes are normal, taking care of your body and mind is essential for the health of both mother and baby.

Following healthy tips in each trimester supports your baby’s development and reduces the risk of complications. Always consult your midwife or obstetrician if you have concerns.

First Trimester (0–12 Weeks)

The first trimester is a critical stage because your baby’s organs are forming. Good nutrition, rest, and early prenatal care are very important.

Nutrition and Vitamins

  • Take prenatal vitamins daily, especially folic acid
  • Eat small, frequent meals to reduce nausea
  • Include fruits, vegetables, whole grains, eggs, lean meat, and dairy
  • Drink plenty of water to prevent dehydration

Calorie note: Extra calories are usually not needed yet.

Avoid Harmful Substances

  • Avoid alcohol, smoking, and vaping
  • Limit caffeine intake
  • Avoid raw or undercooked food and unpasteurized dairy

Activity and Rest

  • Get enough rest and sleep
  • Do light activities such as walking or stretching
  • Avoid heavy lifting and strenuous exercise
  • Practice light stretching and pelvic floor (Kegel) exercises

Prenatal Care

  • Schedule your first prenatal check-up
  • Follow medical advice on supplements and tests
  • Report severe vomiting, bleeding, or pain immediately

Second Trimester (13–26 Weeks)

The second trimester is often called the “comfortable stage” because energy levels improve and baby growth becomes more noticeable.

Nutrition and Weight Gain

  • Eat a balanced diet with slightly increased calories
  • Include calcium-rich foods for bone development
  • Eat iron-rich foods to prevent anemia
  • Continue prenatal vitamins

Calorie note: About 340 extra calories per day for most women.

Physical Activity

  • Walking, swimming, or prenatal yoga (with doctor approval)
  • Practice pelvic floor (Kegel) exercises
  • Maintain good posture and wear supportive shoes

Body Care and Monitoring

  • Wear comfortable clothing
  • Sleep on your side for better circulation
  • Attend regular prenatal check-ups
  • Monitor weight and blood pressure

Third Trimester (27–40 Weeks)

The third trimester focuses on comfort, preparation, and readiness for birth.

Nutrition and Hydration

  • Eat small, frequent meals to reduce heartburn
  • Continue a balanced diet with protein, fiber, calcium, and iron
  • Drink plenty of water
  • Continue prenatal vitamins

Calorie note: About 450 extra calories per day for most women.

Rest and Comfort

  • Sleep on your left side
  • Use pillows for back and leg support
  • Elevate feet to reduce swelling
  • Take frequent rest breaks

Preparation for Delivery

  • Prepare your hospital bag early
  • Learn the signs of labor
  • Attend childbirth and breastfeeding classes
  • Create a birth plan with your healthcare provider

Healthy Habits for the Entire Pregnancy

  • Eat nutritious and balanced meals daily
  • Drink enough water
  • Take prenatal vitamins as prescribed
  • Attend all prenatal check-ups
  • Avoid alcohol, smoking, and illegal drugs
  • Care for your mental and emotional health
  • Avoid stressful situations
