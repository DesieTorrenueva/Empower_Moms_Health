Danger Signs Awareness

During pregnancy, some symptoms are common and not usually a cause for alarm. However, if you experience any symptoms that concern you, contact your midwife or obstetrician immediately.

Always trust your instincts — it is always better to be safe.

Seek Immediate Medical Help If:

  • You are bleeding from the vagina or have severe stomach pain
  • Your baby’s usual pattern of movement has changed
  • You have a severe headache with blurred vision, upper stomach pain, nausea, or swelling
  • You feel extremely emotionally distressed or your mental health feels in crisis
  • One of your calves is red, swollen, and hot
  • You have pain when peeing, fever, or need to urinate more often than usual
  • Any stomach pain feels like tightening

Common Warning Signs

It can sometimes be difficult to tell the difference between a normal pregnancy symptom and a warning sign. Always speak to your healthcare provider if you are worried.

  • Bleeding from the vagina
  • Stomach pain
  • Shortness of breath or chest pain
  • Swollen ankles, feet, or fingers
  • Mental health or emotional distress
  • Change in your baby’s normal movements

Bleeding from the Vagina

Vaginal bleeding can be common during pregnancy and does not always mean there is a problem. However, it can sometimes be a dangerous sign depending on the stage of pregnancy.

First Trimester (0–12 Weeks)

  • Implantation bleeding (spotting)
  • Hormonal changes affecting the cervix
  • Threatened miscarriage
  • Miscarriage
  • Ectopic pregnancy
  • Molar pregnancy

Second & Third Trimester (13–40 Weeks)

  • Late miscarriage (12–23 weeks)
  • Cervical infection or inflammation
  • Placenta praevia
  • Placental abruption
  • Signs of labor or premature labor (before 37 weeks)

Stomach Pain

Mild stomach pain is common, especially in early pregnancy, due to hormonal changes and the growing womb.

  • Urinary tract infection
  • Constipation or trapped wind
  • Premature or preterm labor (before 37 weeks)

Always consult your healthcare provider if stomach pain is severe or persistent.

Change in Baby’s Movements

Contact your midwife or obstetrician immediately if you notice any change in your baby’s movements.

  • Your baby may be unwell
  • Low amniotic fluid
  • Risk of stillbirth

Shortness of Breath or Chest Pain

Feeling short of breath is common in pregnancy, especially in the third trimester. However, chest pain should always be checked immediately.
