Pregnancy Care Features

Pregnancy is a critical period in life. When pregnant, both mother and baby face health risks, such as miscarriage and maternal death during pregnancy, as well as birth defects or congenital abnormalities in the fetus.

Antenatal care is therefore very necessary to help identify risks before pregnancy, during pregnancy, on the delivery date, and after delivery. Proper care ensures that every stage of pregnancy is safe and free from complications, allowing both mother and baby to receive timely treatment when needed.

Pregnancy Symptoms and Signs

Pregnancy causes significant hormonal changes in the body, which can lead to a wide range of physical and emotional symptoms. Every pregnancy is differentâ€”some women experience many symptoms, while others notice only a few.

Early Pregnancy Symptoms

Common symptoms in the early stages of pregnancy may include:

  • Missed menstrual period
  • Breast tenderness or enlargement
  • Fatigue or unusual tiredness
  • Frequent urination
  • Nausea and vomiting (often called morning sickness, which may occur at any time of day)

These symptoms can also be caused by stress, illness, or other factors and do not always mean you are pregnant. If you suspect pregnancy, take a home pregnancy test and consult your GP for confirmation.

Changes in Later Pregnancy

As pregnancy progresses, your body may continue to change. Common symptoms in later stages include:

  • Backache or headaches
  • Leg cramps or varicose veins
  • Itching or tingling sensations
  • Constipation, haemorrhoids, or indigestion
  • Vaginal discharge or vaginitis
  • Mood changes or feelings of low mood or depression

When to See Your GP

If you have any concerns at any stage of pregnancy, do not hesitate to speak with your GP. Seek medical care immediately if you experience:

  • Vaginal bleeding or leaking of fluid (breaking waters)
  • Persistent or severe pain
  • High fever
  • Severe headaches or vision changes
