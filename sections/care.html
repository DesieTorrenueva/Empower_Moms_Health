Pregnancy Care Features

Pregnancy is a critical period in life. During pregnancy, both the mother and the unborn baby face potential health risks such as miscarriage, maternal death, and congenital abnormalities.

Antenatal care (ANC) is essential in identifying and managing risks before, during, and after pregnancy. It ensures that every stage of pregnancy is safe and that both mother and baby receive timely care when needed.

Early Registration of Pregnancy

  • Purpose: Enables early identification of maternal and fetal risks
  • Timing: Within the first 12 weeks of gestation
  • Benefits: Accurate pregnancy dating, baseline health checks, and early education
  • Philippine Context: DOH promotes early registration through Barangay Health Stations and Rural Health Units

Regular Antenatal Check-ups

  • WHO Recommendation: At least 8 ANC visits
  • Schedule: Monthly until 28 weeks, biweekly until 36 weeks, weekly until delivery
  • Purpose: Monitor fetal growth and detect complications such as preeclampsia and gestational diabetes

Health Assessment and Monitoring

  • Blood pressure monitoring
  • Weight gain assessment
  • Uterine fundal height measurement
  • Fetal heart rate monitoring
  • Checking for edema, anemia, or infection

Laboratory Examinations

  • Initial Tests: CBC, urinalysis, blood typing, VDRL/RPR, HIV, Hepatitis B
  • Follow-up Tests: OGTT for gestational diabetes, repeat hemoglobin and urinalysis

Nutritional Care and Supplementation

  • Iron and folic acid supplementation
  • Calcium to reduce preeclampsia risk
  • Vitamin A and multivitamins
  • Dietary counseling using local and affordable food options

Health Education and Counseling

  • Physiological changes during pregnancy
  • Warning signs such as bleeding and severe headache
  • Birth preparedness and emergency planning
  • Breastfeeding and newborn care
  • Family planning and postpartum contraception

Risk Assessment and Early Detection

  • Hypertension, diabetes, anemia, and infections
  • Teenage or advanced maternal age
  • Multiple pregnancies
  • Early referral and specialized care when needed

Psychological and Emotional Support

  • Mental health screening for anxiety and depression
  • Identification of domestic violence risks
  • Counseling and referral to mental health professionals
  • Peer support groups

Promotion of Healthy Lifestyle

  • Light exercise such as walking and prenatal yoga
  • Adequate rest and sleep
  • Proper hygiene and oral care
  • Avoidance of smoking, alcohol, and drugs
  • Participation in DOH programs such as Buntis Congress

Fetal Well-Being Monitoring

  • Maternal monitoring of fetal movements
  • Ultrasound assessment of growth and anatomy
  • Doppler studies for high-risk pregnancies
  • Early detection of fetal distress or growth restriction

Referral System

  • Triggers include severe anemia, preeclampsia, bleeding, and labor complications
  • Coordination between Barangay Health Workers, midwives, and hospitals
  • Ensures access to emergency obstetric and neonatal care (EmONC)
